Case study hook
Look at the feed for 5 seconds.
Then close your eyes. How many posts mentioned hiring? Did you see the layoff? What color was the second profile photo? How many notification badges were there?
You have neurotypical attention and you still missed most of it. This is what ADHD and CDS users navigate every time they open the app.
Annotated costs
1
Notification badge anchoring. Red counts prime hypervigilance. ADHD users disproportionately route attention to alert-colored UI (Pessoa, threat-detection bias).
2
Tab badges with 99+ counts. Forces a "something is unfinished" feeling. Triggers task-switching impulses for ADHD; freezes CDS users in indecision.
3
"+ Follow" CTA inside the post header. A decision injected before you have read a single word. Three CTAs sit on this card alone (Follow, dismiss, See more).
4
"See more" truncation. The first three lines are rarely a summary, they are a hook. Forces a sunk-cost decision: commit or skip without knowing what you are committing to.
5
Reaction count and four action buttons. "2,847 reactions" anchors social proof before evaluation. Four CTAs (Like, Comment, Repost, Send) compete for the next move.
6
Sponsored content mid-feed. Laid out the same as organic content. Forces a context-switch between trust modes (social vs commercial).
7
Emotionally heavy post next to an ad. A layoff post (18K reactions) follows a vendor pitch. The whiplash in tone taxes both ADHD (emotional dysregulation) and CDS (slow context re-orientation).
8
Horizontal "People you may know." Introduces a second scroll axis. Multiple "+ Connect" decisions, each needing social calibration. No off-ramp from the feed.
Eight costs in a single screen of content. The feed continues below, with no natural completion state.
