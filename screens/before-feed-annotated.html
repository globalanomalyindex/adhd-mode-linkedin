CASE STUDY HOOK
Look at the feed for 5 seconds.
Then close your eyes. How many posts mentioned hiring? Did you see the layoff? What color was the second profile photo? How many notification badges were there?
You have neurotypical attention and you still missed most of it. This is what ADHD and CDS users navigate every time they open the app.
ANNOTATED COSTS
1
Notification badge anchoring. Red dots prime hypervigilance. ADHD users disproportionately route attention to alert-colored UI (Pessoa, threat-detection bias).
2
Tab badges with 99+ counts. Forces a "something is unfinished" feeling. Triggers task-switching impulses for ADHD; freezes CDS users in indecision.
3
"+ Follow" CTA inside post header. A decision injected before you've read a single word. Three CTAs visible on this card alone (Follow, dismiss, See more).
4
"See more" truncation. The first 3 lines are rarely a summary, they're a hook. Forces a sunk-cost decision: commit or skip without knowing what you're committing to.
5
Autoplay video with view count. Motion captures involuntary attention (well-established perceptual bias). View count anchors judgment before content is processed.
6
Reaction count + 4 action buttons. "2,847 reactions" anchors social proof before evaluation. 4 ambiguous CTAs (Like, Comment, Repost, Send) compete for the next move.
7
Sponsored content mid-feed. Indistinguishable in layout from organic content. Forces context-switch between trust modes (social vs commercial).
8
Emotionally heavy post adjacent to ad. A layoff announcement (18K reactions) immediately follows a vendor pitch. Whiplash in tone is taxing for both ADHD (emotional dysregulation) and CDS (slow context re-orientation).
9
Horizontal-scroll "People you may know." Introduces a second scroll axis. Multiple "+ Connect" decisions, each requiring social calibration. No off-ramp from the feed.
Nine costs in a single screen of content. The feed continues infinitely below, with no natural completion state.
